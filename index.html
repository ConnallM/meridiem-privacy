Meridiem is built so your health data never leaves your control. This policy explains, in plain language, exactly what the app touches and where it goes.

The short version. Everything Meridiem calculates stays on your device. There are no accounts, no ads, no analytics, and no servers of ours — because we don't run any. Your skin type, exposure history, and limits sync only through your own private iCloud. We never see your data, and we never sell or share it.

1. Who we are

Meridiem ("the app") is developed and published by Connall Milberry ("we", "us", "our"). If you have any questions about this policy or your privacy, contact us at connall@milberry.net.

2. Our approach: on-device by design

Meridiem is a local-first app. The exposure engine runs entirely on your Apple Watch and iPhone. We do not operate any servers, we do not have a backend, and there is no Meridiem account to create. Data you might reasonably consider sensitive — your skin type, your sun-exposure history, your daily limits — is stored on your device and, if you use iCloud, in your own private Apple iCloud storage. It is never transmitted to us.

3. Data the app uses

To do its job, Meridiem reads the following on your device. In each case the data is used only to calculate and display your sun exposure and related metrics.

Health & fitness data — our commitment

Meridiem's use of Apple HealthKit data is governed by Apple's requirements. We want to be explicit:

  • We never use health or fitness data for advertising or marketing.
  • We never sell health or fitness data, or share it with data brokers, advertising networks, or any third party.
  • Health data is used solely to provide the app's features on your device.
  • Meridiem reads from Health only. It does not write any data into Apple Health.

4. Third-party services

Meridiem does not embed any third-party analytics, advertising, tracking, or crash-reporting software. The only external services it relies on are provided by Apple as part of the operating system:

  • Apple WeatherKit — to fetch the UV index and forecast for your location. Your location is sent to Apple to return the weather. This is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy and WeatherKit terms.
  • Apple iCloud — if enabled on your device, your profile and history sync across your own devices through your private iCloud. This is Apple infrastructure tied to your Apple Account; we have no access to it. Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

We receive nothing from these services about you.

5. Notifications

Meridiem can send you local notifications — for example, a heads-up before you reach your daily limit, an extreme-UV warning, or a sunscreen reapply reminder during a sun session. These are scheduled and delivered entirely on your device. No notification content is sent through any server of ours.

6. What we collect: nothing

We do not collect, receive, store, or have access to any personal data, usage data, or health data. Because Meridiem has no backend and no analytics, there is simply no channel through which your information could reach us. For Apple's App Privacy questionnaire, Meridiem's data is not collected by the developer and not used to track you.

7. Data retention and deletion

Because your data lives on your device (and optionally in your iCloud), you are always in control:

  • Delete on-device data by deleting the app. This removes Meridiem's local storage.
  • Delete synced data by managing Meridiem's data in Settings → [your name] → iCloud on your device.
  • Revoke access to Health or Location at any time in the iOS Settings app; the app continues to run with reduced functionality.

8. Children's privacy

Meridiem is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect information from children. As described above, we do not collect personal information from anyone.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under laws such as the GDPR (EU/UK) or the CCPA (California) to access, correct, or delete your personal data. Because we hold no personal data about you, there is nothing for us to retrieve, correct, or erase on our side — you already hold and control all of it on your own device and iCloud. We do not sell personal information.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, note the change in the app or its App Store release notes. Continued use of Meridiem after a change means you accept the revised policy.

11. Contact
